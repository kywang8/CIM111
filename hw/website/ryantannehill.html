Everything You Need to Know About Ryan Tannehill

As the starting Quarterback (QB) for the Miami Dolphins, Ryan Tannehill has racked up an impressive resume, starting with his college career at Texas A&M, where he originally played Wide Reciever (WR). Taken 8th in the 2012 NFL draft, Tannehill was the Miami Dolphins' first pick, and the 3rd QB taken in that draft behind only Andrew Luck and Robert Griffin III.

Biography

Ryan Tannehill was born in Lubbock, Texas in 1988. He played multiple positions on the Big Spring High School football team, including QB, Defensive Back (DB), and Punter (P). Along with football, Tannehill also competed on the school's basketball and track and field teams. He earned his BA in biology from Texas A&M, and had plans to be an orthopedic surgeon prior to his NFL career. His wife, Lauren Ashley Tannehill, met Ryan in Panama City in 2009 and the couple tied the knot in early 2012. In August 2016, Lauren gave birth to Steel Ryan Tannehill.

Contract Details

Three months after the 2012 draft, Tannehill signed his rookie contract for a total of $12.67 million over 4 years. In May of 2016, the Dolphins extended Tannehill's contract for a total of 4 years and $77M. Last year Tannehill earned $9.34M in base salary, and he is set to earn $17.975M in 2017. He is signed through the 2020 season, and will be an unrestricted free agent in 2021 if the Dolphins do not extend his contract. His 4 year extension is worth a total of $77M with $45M guaranteed and an average salary of $19.25M a year.

College Career

At Texas A&M, Tannehill started off battling for the starting QB position, but eventually made the switch to WR. As a freshman, Tannehill led the team in receptions and receiving yards and set freshman records for the school. He was voted Offensive MVP by his teammates. He continued his career as a WR in his sophomore year, where he saw action in all 13 games, including 3 at QB, where he was backup. In his junior season, Tannehill started the final 6 games as QB, earning a school record 65.0 completion percentage and 5 wins. Carrying a 3.60 GPA in biology, Tannehill was named the ESPN Academic All-District team and the Academic All-Big 12 football team. Throughout the 2011 season, Tannehill started all 13 games for the Aggies at QB and also served as team captain. Despite a mediocre season for the team, which went 7-6, they were invited to a bowl game against Northwestern which they won. At the end of Tannehill's career at Texas A&M he had racked up a total of 5,450 passing yards, 42 touchdown passes, and 21 interceptions.

Professional Career

After finalizing his rookie contract in 2012, Tannehill was pronounced the starter for that season. His first game as a Dolphin ended in a disappointing 30-10 loss versus the Houston Texans. Tannehill's performance remained spotty throughout the season, but he managed to break Dolphins rookie records for most passing completions, passing attempts, and passing yards. He also had the longest rush of a Dolphins rookie QB. However, the Dolphins ended the season wth a mediocre 7-9 record.
These types of performances became commonplace for Tannehill, who alternated between high and low levels of play seemingly with no rhyme or reason. His second and third seasons were similar, ending in two consecutive 8-8 records for the Dolphins.
In 2015, despite general criticism from fans, Tannehill signed a 6-year, $96M contract. Even with better play from the QB, the Dolphins ended 6-10. In a victory over the Houston Texans that season, Tannehill became the 64th QB in NFL history to post a perfect 158.3 passer rating, as well as completing an NFL record 25 consecutive passes. HC Joe Philbin was fired four games into the season after starting with a 1-3 record. His interim replacement was tight ends coach Dan Campbell, who started promisingly with a 2-0 record, but ultimately ended 5-7.
In 2016, Adam Gase was hired and the coaching staff underwent major changes, including letting go of OC Bill Lazor. That season saw major improvement from Tannehill, who was allowed more leeway to audible and act as both QB and game manager. After a disappointing 1-4 start, the Dolphins rallied and won the next 6 games straight. They clinched a winning record for the first time since 2008, and also made the playoffs. Unfortunately, Tannehill went down in Week 14 with a knee injury, and his dreams of a playoff trip were cut short. Matt Moore started against the Pittsburgh Steelers in that Wildcard round, which ended in a 12-30 loss. The same ACL injury kept Tannehill out for the entire 2017 season.
Under Gase, Tannehill has improved immensely, showing better leadership skills and athletic abilities. His incredibly accurate deep balls were a pleasant surprise for Fins' fans, and the young WR core he throws to continues to show promise.

Injuries

Prior to the 2016-17 season, Tannehill had never missed a game despite consistently recording high numbers of sacks. In Week 14 of last year, Arizona Cardinals defensive end Calais Campbell collided with Tannehill's leg, injuring his knee. Initially, there was concern that his ACL was torn, but further testing revealed that the QB had only sprained his ACL and MCL. He did not require surgery, and according to the Miami Herald on February 23rd, Tannehill’s knee was “pronounced healed by team doctor John Uribe and with a concurring second opinion of respected surgeon Dr. James Andrews." Tannehill was a full participant in all offseason activities leading up to the 2017-18 season, although he had been wearing a knee brace as a precaution. In pre-season, he tweaked the injury again and it was decided he should rest for the season instead of chancing further injury. Therefore, he did not play at all in the 2017-2018 season.
